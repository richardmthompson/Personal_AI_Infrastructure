## Section 5: Reference - Decision Frameworks

### When to Create Anchor vs Derivative

**Create Anchor Content when**:
- You have 3-4 hours for deep work
- You've solved a complex problem worth comprehensive explanation
- You want to establish expertise on a topic
- You're at a major project milestone
- Beginning of month (Weeks 1-2)

**Create Derivative Content when**:
- You have 30-60 min for content creation
- You want to maximize reach of existing anchor content
- You're in Week 3-4 of month (repurposing phase)
- You need quick wins for consistency
- You're low on creative energy

**Anchor â†’ Derivative Ratio**:
- 1 anchor piece â†’ 10+ derivatives (optimal repurposing)
- See [WORKFLOW.md](WORKFLOW.md) for specific repurposing model

### Which Pillar to Pull From

**Check these in order**:

1. **This month's emphasis** (from [MONTH]-PLAN.md)
   - If month is "Alpha Sprint", Pillar 1 (project focus) emphasized

2. **Your current work** (document what you're building)
   - Building voice feature â†’ Pillar 1 (Voice-First)
   - Researching context engineering â†’ Pillar 2 (Cognitive Architecture)
   - Building agents â†’ Pillar 3 (Agentic Systems)

3. **Audience response** (what's resonating)
   - Check last week's analytics - which pillar posts performed best?

4. **Baseline percentages** (from [CONTENT-STRATEGY.md](CONTENT-STRATEGY.md))
   - If you've been heavy on one pillar, balance with another

5. **Your energy level** (sustainability)
   - High energy â†’ Technical deep-dive (Pillars 1-3)
   - Low energy â†’ Reflection/learning journey (Pillar 5)

### When to Adjust Strategy

**Adjust monthly** (minor tweaks):
- Pillar emphasis based on project focus
- Posting frequency based on capacity
- Platform mix based on time available

**Adjust quarterly** (strategic shifts):
- Pillar baseline percentages if audience preferences clear
- Add/remove platform if ROI data compelling
- Workflow changes if efficiency gains proven

**Adjust rarely** (major pivots):
- Core philosophy (IMPLEMENTATION-GUIDE.md)
- Complete pillar restructure
- Fundamental business model change

**RED FLAGS - Adjust immediately**:
- Burnout signals (dreading content creation)
- Declining quality to meet frequency
- Zero engagement for 4+ weeks straight
- Severe negative feedback

### Red Flags to Watch

**Burnout indicators**:
- Resenting time spent on content
- Quality declining noticeably
- Dreading opening social media apps
- Constant comparison / "why aren't I growing faster?"

**Strategy misalignment**:
- Creating content about topics you don't work on
- Pillar mix doesn't match actual development work
- Audience asking for different content than you provide

**Efficiency problems**:
- Time per post increasing (should decrease over time)
- No content buffer (always scrambling day-of)
- Repurposing not working (recreating from scratch)

**Engagement issues**:
- Declining engagement rate despite consistent posting
- Zero meaningful conversations/DMs
- Content feels like shouting into void (> 3 months)

## Section 6: Troubleshooting

### Common Issues & Solutions

#### Issue: "I don't have time for content creation"

**Reality check**: With full-time job, 5-7 hours/week is realistic

**Solutions**:
1. **Reduce frequency**: 1-2 posts/week maintains presence
2. **Document, don't create**: Record your normal work, share it (minimal extra time)
3. **Batch ruthlessly**: 2 hours Sunday to create week's content
4. **Lower quality bar**: "Good enough" beats perfect
5. **Repurpose everything**: 1 blog post â†’ 10 tweets, 1 newsletter, 3 LinkedIn posts

**Time optimization tactics**:
- Voice-to-text for drafting (faster than typing)
- AI for first drafts (Claude/ChatGPT), you edit
- Templates for repetitive formats (save in [WORKFLOW.md](WORKFLOW.md))
- Schedule everything (never post manually)

**Check [WORKFLOW.md](WORKFLOW.md)** for batching system details

---

#### Issue: "My content isn't getting engagement"

**Common causes & solutions**:

1. **Too early** (First 20-50 posts get little engagement)
   - Solution: Keep posting, 3-month minimum before judging
   - Building algorithm trust + audience takes time

2. **Wrong timing** (Timezone misalignment or off-peak hours)
   - Solution: Use Typefully's suggested times, experiment
   - Best times (US audience): 8-10am ET, 12-1pm ET, 5-6pm ET

3. **Content not valuable** (Too generic, too technical, or not solving problems)
   - Solution: Ask audience what they want to learn
   - Make content more specific and actionable

4. **No engagement from you** (Algorithm punishes one-way broadcasters)
   - Solution: Reply to every comment, engage with 10 others daily
   - See daily engagement tasks in [WEEKLY-TRACKER.md](WEEKLY-TRACKER.md)

5. **Weak hooks** (First sentence doesn't grab attention)
   - Solution: Spend 20% of time on opening line
   - See platform-specific formats in [WORKFLOW.md](WORKFLOW.md)

**Debugging process**:
- Compare your posts to successful creators - what's different?
- Ask 3-5 trusted people for honest feedback
- Post same content at different times (A/B test timing)
- Try different formats (threads vs single posts, text vs images)

---

#### Issue: "I feel like an imposter - who am I to teach?"

**Reality**: 88% of developers experience imposter syndrome

**Mindset shifts**:

1. **You don't need to be an expert to share**
   - Share what you're learning, not what you've mastered
   - "Learning in public" is the whole point
   - Someone 6 months behind you finds your insights valuable

2. **You're not claiming to be the world's best**
   - You're documenting your journey
   - "Here's what worked for me" â‰  "This is the only way"

3. **Vulnerability builds connection**
   - Sharing struggles makes you relatable
   - Perfectionism creates distance, authenticity builds trust

**Action**:
- Frame posts as "Here's what I learned" not "Here's what you should do"
- Share failures alongside wins (see Pillar 5 in [CONTENT-STRATEGY.md](CONTENT-STRATEGY.md))
- Be transparent about your experience level

#### Issue: "I'm getting negative comments / criticism"

**Framework for handling criticism**:

1. **Constructive criticism** ("Your code has a security flaw...")
   - Response: Thank them, fix it, learn from it
   - This is valuable feedback

2. **Misunderstanding** ("You're wrong because [misinterpretation]")
   - Response: Clarify once politely, don't argue if they persist
   - Not worth extended debate

3. **Trolling** ("This is the dumbest thing I've ever read")
   - Response: Ignore completely, or block if persistent
   - Don't engage with bad faith

4. **Hate** (Personal attacks, harassment)
   - Response: Block immediately, report if severe
   - Zero tolerance

**Mental health tips**:
- **Limit exposure**: Check notifications 2-3x/day max
- **Block liberally**: Your mental health > defending yourself to strangers
- **Remember the ratio**: 98% neutral/positive, 2% negative (negativity bias distorts perception)
- **Talk to peers**: Other creators understand, vent in DMs

**If negativity overwhelming**: Take a break. Well-being > content.

#### Issue: "I'm feeling burned out"

**Immediate actions**:

1. **Take a planned break** (1-2 weeks)
   - Announce transparently to audience
   - Schedule content in advance if possible
   - Completely disconnect

2. **Reduce frequency** (sustainability > growth)
   - 3x/week â†’ 2x/week or 1x/week
   - Update [WEEKLY-TRACKER.md](WEEKLY-TRACKER.md) with new cadence

3. **Change format** (refresh creative energy)
   - If writing feels stale, try video or audio
   - Switch primary platform temporarily

4. **Outsource** (budget tier: $200-500/month)
   - Hire editor for polish
   - Designer for graphics
   - VA for scheduling/engagement

5. **Reconnect with why** (check [IMPLEMENTATION-GUIDE.md](IMPLEMENTATION-GUIDE.md))
   - Read "Why Build in Public?" section
   - Remember original motivation
   - Is this still aligned with goals?

**Prevention** (quarterly review in Section 4):
- Build 2-week content buffer
- Schedule breaks proactively (1 week/quarter)
- Monitor burnout signals early
- Adjust before breaking point

#### Issue: "I don't know what's working"

**Set up analytics tracking**:

1. **Create tracking spreadsheet** (Google Sheets or Notion)
   - Columns: Date, Platform, Post, Impressions, Likes, Comments, Shares
   - Track weekly (Friday afternoons)

2. **Weekly review** (15 min every Friday)
   - Top 3 performing posts - what made them work?
   - Engagement rate trends
   - Update [WEEKLY-TRACKER.md](WEEKLY-TRACKER.md) retrospective

3. **Monthly deep dive** (1 hour, first Saturday of month)
   - See Section 3: Monthly Planning, Step 1
   - Identify patterns across full month

**What to track**:
- Engagement rate: (Likes + Comments + Shares) / Impressions
  - 3-5% = good, 5-10% = great, 10%+ = excellent
- Follower growth rate (net new per week)
- Top performing content pillars
- Best posting times for your audience

**What to ignore** (vanity metrics):
- Total follower count (quality > quantity)
- Impressions alone (reach without engagement meaningless)
- Likes only (lowest-effort engagement)
